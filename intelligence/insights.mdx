**Insights** automatically identifies the narratives driving your coverage and explains why they matter.

## What is a narrative?

A narrative is a cluster of related mentions telling the same story — e.g. "pricing backlash on Reddit", "CEO interview tour", "regulatory scrutiny in EU press".

Grapevine groups mentions by semantic similarity, not just keyword overlap, so you see the _story_ rather than a bag of links.

## Insights feed

Each insight card includes:

- **Narrative title** — a concise label for the story
- **Summary** — AI-generated explanation of what's being said
- **Mention count** — how much coverage it represents
- **Sentiment trend** — is the narrative improving or worsening?
- **Top sources** — who's driving the story

Click through to see all mentions in the narrative.

## When to use Insights vs Dashboard

| View | Best for |
| --- | --- |
| **Dashboard** | Daily numbers — volume, reach, sentiment, top stories |
| **Insights** | Understanding _why_ coverage is moving — the stories behind the stats |

## Filtering

Insights respects your topic and channel filters. Narrow to a single brand or social-only to see platform-specific narratives.

<Tip>
  Check Insights after a spike alert on the Dashboard. The alert tells you _that_ something happened; Insights tells you _what_ the story is.
</Tip>

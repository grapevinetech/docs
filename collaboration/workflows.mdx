**Workflows** automate repetitive monitoring tasks — alerting the right people when something happens, without manual inbox checking.

## Example workflows

- Email the comms lead when negative sentiment exceeds 60% on a topic
- Slack notification when a tier-1 outlet mentions your brand
- Create a tagged mention group when volume spikes 3× above baseline
- Trigger an audio call for critical risk signals

## Building a workflow

1. Go to **Workflows** in the sidebar
2. Click **New workflow**
3. Define a **trigger** — mention volume, sentiment threshold, source match, keyword, etc.
4. Add **conditions** — topic, channel, tag filters
5. Choose an **action** — email, notification, tag, assign
6. Activate

## Workflow alerts

When a workflow fires, affected team members receive an alert. Control workflow email delivery in **Settings → Notifications → Workflow alerts**.

## Best practices

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Start simple">
    One trigger, one action. Add complexity once you've validated the workflow catches what you expect.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Name clearly">
    "Negative news alert — Brand topic" beats "Workflow 3" when you're debugging at 2am.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Test with a narrow scope">
    Run new workflows on a single topic first before applying workspace-wide.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

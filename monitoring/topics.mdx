## Using the topic builder

Go to **Topics** in the sidebar. Under **"What do you want to monitor?"**:

1. **Name** *(optional)* — a label for dashboards and filters (`Federal budget deal`). If left blank, Grapevine derives a name from your keywords or themes.
2. **Keywords** — comma-separated terms (`senate, shutdown, @capitolstrategy`). A mention matches if it contains any term in the group. Use **+ Add AND group** to require additional keyword sets (e.g. `senate` AND `appropriations`).
3. **Themes** — optional plain-language subjects (`politics, federal budget`). Grapevine maps these to news themes and expands them across channels. At least one keyword or theme is required.
4. **Advanced** — open to set:
   - **Excludes** — terms to filter out
   - **Sources** — limit to specific outlets or channels
   - **History** — how far back to pull when the topic starts (then it updates continuously)
   - **Language** and **Region**
   - Sentiment/summary guidance and custom scoring questions
5. Click **Create topic**. Grapevine begins collecting immediately; open the topic card to see mentions, analytics, and settings.

Your active topics appear below in **Your topics** — each card shows keyword badges, mention count, and an **Open** link.

## Keyword strategy

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Brand monitoring">
    Include your brand name, common misspellings, product names, and `@` handles. Add your CEO or spokesperson if they're part of the narrative.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Competitor tracking">
    Create a separate topic per competitor. Keep keywords tight — the competitor's name and flagship products, not generic industry terms.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Issue or campaign tracking">
    Use the campaign name, hashtag, product launch codename, and key phrases from your messaging. Remove or archive the topic when the campaign ends.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

<Tip>
  Review keyword performance after a week. If you're drowning in noise, remove broad terms. If you're missing coverage, add alternate spellings and nicknames — or add a **theme** to catch related coverage the keywords miss.
</Tip>

## Topic settings

Open a topic and use the **⋯** menu → **Edit settings** to adjust keywords, themes, sources, history, and alerts.

## Deleting a topic

Deleting a topic removes it from monitoring. Historical mentions remain in your workspace until they age out per your plan's retention window.

<Warning>
  Deletion is permanent for the topic configuration. Export any reports you need before deleting.
</Warning>
